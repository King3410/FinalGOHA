FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)

When does enrollment for the upcoming school year start and end?
Please see the attached files below to download our complete institutional schedules.
Can I enroll my child online, or do I need to come to the school?
Yes, you need to personally come to the school campus to process the admission documentation.
Do you accept transferees or late enrollees?
Yes. We welcome and support students with special needs as part of our inclusive education approach. A recommendation from a pediatrician or specialist may be required to ensure proper placement and appropriate mainstreaming support.
What are the age requirements for Kindergarten and Grade 1?
The age requirement for entering Kindergarten is 5 years old; for Grade 1, the requirement is 6 years old.
How much is the tuition fee for each grade level?
Please download the official resource attachment file located below for a full financial breakdown.
Do you offer discounts for siblings enrolled in the same school?
Yes, we offer special sibling discounts. We also provide financial incentives for students who actively represent the academy and participate in official academic events.
Are school uniforms required, and where can we buy them?
Yes, students are strictly required to wear their assigned official school uniforms inside the campus. You can purchase them directly from our school store.
Are there any miscellaneous or hidden fees we should know about?
No, we do not have any hidden fees. All academic and institutional charges are clearly detailed in our breakdown sheet available for download below.
Do you accept students with special needs?
Yes, we warmly welcome and support special needs students. We aim to construct an inclusive space and cooperate continuously with parents to support individual developmental goals.
Are there installment payment options or scholarships available?
Yes, installment structures are accessible under strict payment deadlines. Merit-based scholarships are also granted to students who maintain excellent academic performance and represent the school.
